We, the students, need to speak up.

Lexington Public Schools is making a decision that will affect our day-to-day lives—let's make sure they hear what we have to say.

The Lexington Public Schools are making plans for us to go back to school—but they don't know what we think. You might’ve heard about this plan from your parents, from your friends—yet the district hasn't directly asked our opinion. So, now is our time to be heard. Each week we’re writing a letter to the Lexington School Committee with our thoughts and questions.

Each week on Saturday a letter will be generated and sent to the Committee.

Read our letters Read the district's plan

How you can engage:

1. Rank from 1 to 5

We've created a list of agree-disagree statements to poll your opinion on the school's plan. All you need to do is rank each statement based on how much you agree from 1 → 5. We'll compile the results of this poll into our letter to the School Committee. It should take under 2 minutes.

2. Open response

If you have time, we'd really appreciate any extended thoughts that you have on LPS' COVID plan. Two sentences or an essay—totally fine. We may be able to add other mediums if they can be diplayed with some sort of text like a link. These will compose the large majority of our letters to the School Committee.
