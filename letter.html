Dear Ms. Vincent,

We’re Theo, Luke, and Saharsh—three Westborough High School school students who have been closely watching the district build its plan for the upcoming school year and were increasingly concerned that the student body wouldn’t have a voice.

So, last Monday, we released a poll asking middle school and high school students what they thought of the plan. After collecting 143 voluntary responses from students, we crunched the numbers. Students were asked whether they agreed or disagreed with 8 statements on a scale of 1 to 5.

We used a “statistical inference test,” which is where we draw a conclusion (i.e: the majority of students believe x) based on what students think about each question. We considered >50% a majority. We’ve also attached the raw data (with student names anonymized) below in case you’d like to take a look.

One-hundred and forty-three students responded to the poll—here’s what they think:

  • Students are unsure whether they feel comfortable going to school 2-3 days a week.
  • A majority of students are against any plan that involves going to school 5 days a week.
  • A majority of students are OK with some implementation of going to school in-person.
  • A majority of students would feel comfortable if there were more than two groups (Navy & Cardinal) of students
  • A majority of students would prefer to go to school for two days in a row, then take two days off instead of going every other day
  • A majority of students are comfortable having 9-12 peers in their classroom
  • A majority of students aren’t comfortable with having 7 hours a day (high school) & 5 hours a day (middle school) of work on non-school days
    • Our recommendation (based on the data) would be to lower this number. This is a stressful time, and students should be able to use their days off to decompress as well as complete work.
  • A majority of students are comfortable with being taught by a mix of teacher from Westborough & neighboring towns online.

Eight students also provided written responses (attached). We’ve attached some highlights below:

“as much as it saddens me to say it, I have to. it isn't going to work, one missed COVID case will turn into hundreds in a matter of weeks. I cannot imagine a scenario that is completely air-tight, it can never be. it simply will not work, and not particularly worried about myself rather than the teachers that see many kids in one day and those who have to clean the building frequently. they are at risk and I do not want to be part of the problem.”

—A rising junior at WHS

“I noticed that one of the statements in the poll was "I would prefer going to school in-person for two days, then taking two days off—rather than going every other day.” and I completely agree with it. As a student, I would feel much more comfortable going into school the second day in a week knowing that there wouldn’t have been the entire other half of the school (for WHS, some 500-600 people!) that would have walked the same halls and sat in the same desks. I feel that we could also get more learning done this way. It is a longer time to see teachers between blocks, but there isn’t an interruption after every day which might end up being a greater disruption. Two day blocks would be particularly helpful for the labs and projects in science and science-related classes, as well as any group projects in any class. Additionally, instead of how Fridays are currently set up, I think that it could be added to the two day block of Wednesday and Thursday to further minimize risk as the same students will be passing through school for three days at a time. Of course, all this is mentioned considering that I would rather be taught by Westborough teachers live remotely. However, that is not the current remote plan, so while I dislike the hybrid plan, to me it is the least bad of the choices which are all unpalatable.”

—A rising junior at WHS

We sincerely hope that you’ll take this information into consideration. We also kindly request that you acknowledge this letter at the next School Committee meeting and provide your thoughts.

Thanks in advance for your time & we hope you’re well.

Sincerely,

Theo Bleier (11th), Luke Carapezza (12th), Saharsh Yeruva (12th)
